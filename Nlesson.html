How to Properly Use Conjunctions

What is a conjunction and what are the types of conjunctions?

A conjunction links independent and dependent clauses together. There are three types of conjunctions.

Coordinating Conjunctions: For, and, nor, but, or, yet, so. Also known as FANBOYS.

Subordinating Conjunctions: Although, after, before, If, when, whether, while.

Correlative Conjunctions: Connects two elements that are grammatically correct. For example, neither, nor, either, or and both.

It is important to know the proper time use of conjunctions, since they are often used incorrectly. There must always be a correct use of a comma and there must also be a relationship between the independent and dependent clause.

Whether it is a similar clause or a cause and effect use of the conjunction. Conjunctions also are a great way to avoid comma splices as well.
